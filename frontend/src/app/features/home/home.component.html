Perpetual Wealth

What does it take to never work again — mathematically?

Perpetual Wealth is a precision financial modelling tool that answers a deceptively simple question: given your spending, your investment returns, your tax rate, and inflation, how much initial wealth do you need so that your portfolio sustains you indefinitely — with net wealth neither growing nor shrinking?

Unlike rule-of-thumb calculators, this tool uses exact symbolic mathematics. The real after-tax return rate is computed precisely as r = (1 + i(1 − T)) / (1 + π) − 1, not approximated. Closed-form solutions to the governing differential equations give you exact wealth trajectories over time. Sensitivity analysis tells you, to the dollar, how much each variable affects your required initial wealth.

Choose a mode below to begin. Deterministic models give exact answers under fixed assumptions. Stochastic models — coming soon — will introduce randomness to return rates and simulate probability distributions of outcomes.
