Perpetual Wealth

What does it take to never work again — mathematically?

Perpetual Wealth is a fun, hypothetical modelling tool that explores a deceptively simple question: given your spending, your investment returns, your tax rate, and inflation, how much initial wealth would you need so that your portfolio sustains you indefinitely — with net wealth neither growing nor shrinking?

It operates in a simplified, idealised world. Parameters are assumed constant, compounding is instantaneous, and taxes are a flat rate on returns. Real life is messier — markets fluctuate, tax codes are complicated, and governments change. This tool is not a financial plan. It is a mathematical thought experiment.

Within that idealised world, the mathematics is exact. The real after-tax return rate is computed precisely as r = (1 + i(1 − T)) / (1 + π) − 1, not approximated. Closed-form solutions to the governing differential equations give exact wealth trajectories. Sensitivity analysis shows, to the dollar, how each variable affects the required initial wealth under the model's assumptions.

Choose a mode below to begin. Deterministic models give exact answers under fixed assumptions. Stochastic models introduce year-to-year randomness and simulate probability distributions of outcomes across thousands of paths.
